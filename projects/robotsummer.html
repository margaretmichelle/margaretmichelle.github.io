Robot Summer

| ENPH 253 | 2022 |

Overview:

During the summer after the second year of Engineering Physics, there is a themed robot competition where classmates form teams of four, design and build an autonomous robot in a couple of months, and compete against each other; our year's theme was treasure hunting, Uncharted-style. An obstacle course was created with a variety of challenges and idols to steal. (Full details can be found here.) My team designed and built a fully autonomous, wheeled robot that made it to the quarter-finals and competed in an sudden-death match against the winning team. We modelled, laser-cut, 3D printed, and assembled the chassis; designed, soldered, and tested all our circuits; developed, integrated, tested, and optimized our C++ code until we had a robot that could consistently line follow, retrieve idols, and overcome obstacles.

Rapid prototyping Onshape Laser cutting 3D printing Circuit design and troubleshooting Soldering STM32 Blue Pill

Robot Functions:

  • Line following using IR photoelectric sensors and PID
  • Object detection using ultrasonic sensors
  • Object pickup, including self-positioning and dropping into storage basket
  • Magnet detection using Hall effect sensors
  • Bridge deployment to cross a chasm


My Contributions:

  • Designed the claw in Onshape and laser cut from acrylic sheets
  • Organized GPIO pin assignment for two STM32 microcontrollers (acting as master and slave) connected to the motors, sensors, and power
  • Programmed object detection and claw operation
  • Assisted in programming the navigation system including interrupt handlers and PID control
  • Created a UI to see real-time sensor data and modify PID coefficients to facilitate and expedite testing
  • Designed and soldered circuits for motor driving and photoelectric sensor signal filtering
  • Conducted full system testing
